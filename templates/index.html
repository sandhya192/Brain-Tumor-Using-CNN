Tumor Classification Types

Four distinct categories with clinical significance

Glioma

Brain tumors arising from glial cells. Most common primary brain tumor in adults. Requires immediate medical attention.

High Priority

Meningioma

Tumors forming on membranes covering the brain and spinal cord. Usually benign but requires monitoring.

Moderate Priority

Pituitary

Tumors in the pituitary gland affecting hormone production and vision. Treatable with proper diagnosis.

Hormonal Impact

No Tumor

Normal brain MRI scan with no detected abnormalities or tumor presence. Healthy tissue identified.

Healthy
